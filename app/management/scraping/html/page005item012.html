 Description: Federal City Recovery Services (FCRS) currently provides transitional housing for men and women in sites throughout Washington, DC. When FCRS was established, it was incorporated to provide transitional housing and to further address the substance abuse treatment needs of those who have issues with the criminal justice system. With the Access to Recovery (ATR) Program, FCRS works with DC Pretrial Services and the Department of Justice to provide a complete continuum of care for men, women and families in Pretrial status, and/or on Parole or Probation. FCRS also provides transitional recovery care housing for men and women in three sites throughout the Washington, DC area with a current capacity for 86 people.

FCRS also works closely with the DC Department of Health--APRA program to provide substance abuse treatment aftercare, employment services, case management, recovery counseling, education in three outpatient centers.

Bellevue Transitional Housing is a component of Federal City Recovery Services short-term residential program. Male Participants complete 30 days of residential treatment and then proceed to participate in 90 days of transitional housing. There is no direct access to transitional housing unless the placement is done through CSOSA contract. All others must complete the 30 day residential component.
