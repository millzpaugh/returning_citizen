 Description: The Holy Comforter Community Action Group (CAG) was founded by members of The Holy Comforter-St. Cyprian Catholic Church. This faith-based, community and family-oriented organization provides substance abuse treatment, residential transitional housing, and outpatient programs throughout the District of Columbia. CAG offers three main treatment programs: An Outpatient drug treatment facility, a Men’s residential treatment facility, and a Women’s residential treatment facility with a transitional housing component.

The Men’s Residential Treatment Program offers intensive short term (30 day) residential drug treatment 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Certified addictions counselors, case managers, social workers, and other agency professionals collaborate for a multi-disciplinary approach to treatment planning on a monthly or as needed basis in order to assess progress or further intervention needs. Evidence based treatment modalities for relapse prevention, and cognitive behavioral therapy are utilized. The curriculum includes: behavior modification, drug education, relapse prevention, anger management, individual counseling, trauma groups, and life skills training. Trained staff also accompanies clients involved with the criminal justice system to court appearances as well as accompany clients with other mandates to their appointments. Specialized sessions for clients who have been dually-diagnosed are also available.

This facility is metro accessible from the Stadium-Armory station on the Blue/Orange line, as well as several bus routes including but not limited to the 32, 34, 36, 96, 97, All, B2, D6, K11, M6, V8, X2, and X8. Transportation offered in some instances. Call for more details.
