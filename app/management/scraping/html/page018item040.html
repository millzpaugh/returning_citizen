 Description: The Spanish Catholic Center offers many programs that meet the emergency and long term needs of clients. The center has 4 locations and offers holistic services to immigrants around the world through services that include: medical and dental clinics, job training programs, English classes, a food pantry and case management services.

The Spanish Catholic Center offers four unique English as an Other Language (ESOL) programs designed to provide educational resources that will allow non-native English speakers to learn vital life and work skills. All classes to help non-English speakers learn conversational and workplace English are offered at several Spanish Catholic Center locations and require placement test for participation.

The ESOL courses offered focus on assisting non-native English speakers master English as an educational and vocational tool. These courses are offered during 11-week sessions in the spring, summer and fall. Please note that pre-registration is required.

The Family and Children Literacy Program is an extension of the ESOL program and is designed to support parents and their children in an English-speaking environment.

The Spanish Literacy Program, is designed for native Spanish speakers, with aims to increase literacy in Spanish among individuals who may not have received a formal education in their home country.

The Bridge Program focuses on preparing non-native English speakers master English so that they can transition to higher paying jobs or continue their formal education.

Medical Clinics The Spanish Catholic Center offers access to primary care for adults and children. Through the programs, General Medical Services, people of all ages who are low-income and uninsured, or covered by Alliance DC are welcome. The center provides low-cost, patient-centered primary care services and specialty services on-site, including minor surgery, Gynecology, Cardiology, Pulmonology, Nephrology, Dermatology, Orthopedics, Acupuncture and referrals to other specialty services including Physical Therapy.

Additional services offered also include additional program to enhance health care, which include: chronic care initiative, diabetes focus groups, Pasos Saludables (healthy steps), medication assistance programs, ¡celbremos la vida! (cancer prevention), project wish, colorectal cancer education, diabetic eye exams and counseling services.

Appointments can be made by calling (301) 740-2523 and walk-ins are also accepted.
