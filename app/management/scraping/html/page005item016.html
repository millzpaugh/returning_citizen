 Description: First Helping is DC Central Kitchen’s street-level outreach program that uses food as a tool to empower homeless and low-income individuals with the first steps in overcoming hunger, addiction, and unemployment. By providing initial tools, guidance, and advocacy, the outreach goal is to help men and women out of the receiving line and in control of their own lives.

Every morning, the mobile outreach team serves up over 180 hot meals to clients in three different locations in Southeast D.C. While volunteers pass out hot coffee and hearty sandwiches, trained outreach workers engage with nearly 200 clients per day, providing referrals to addiction recovery programs, affordable and supportive housing, mental health support, employment, and access to health care.
