 Description: The Department of Mental Health (DMH) provides services to homeless individuals 18 years or older who have mental illness. Homeless Outreach Teams provide crisis assessment and interventions to homeless persons who may be experiencing a mental health crisis on the street or in a homeless shelter. Outreach teams visit shelters weekly and provide expanded day services at the Hermano Pedro Day Program. DMH also operates a sobering station during hypothermia season.

In addition, DMH helps people who are temporarily or chronically homeless and have persistent mental illness find descent housing where they can be safe and get the support they need, as well as, assist with preventing evictions of individuals receiving mental health services.
