 Description: Miriam’s House became a part of N Street Village in October 2011 and serves homeless women who are living with HIV and AIDS. At Miriam’s House, up to 17 women live together and work on managing and improving their physical health while addressing other self-sufficiency goals, which can include addiction recovery, mental wellness, education, employment, income, and long-term housing. Residents will also have access to all of the programs offered at N Street Village’s main site at 14th and N Streets NW. Each resident has a private bedroom and is expected to pay rent at a rate of 30% of the resident’s income. Residents must have an HIV+ diagnosis and be connected to a primary care physician. Residents are, also required to participate in case management, periodic meetings and activities.

Our shelter and housing programs require an intake, a recent TB test, staff approval, and a signed residential agreement. We only complete intakes when we have spaces open in our programs.
