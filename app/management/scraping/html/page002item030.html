 Description: Central Union Mission is the oldest social service agency in the District of Columbia. Central Union Mission is a faith-based 501(c)3 nonprofit organization with four locations throughout the Washington area. It operates an 18-month rehabilitation program for men with life-controlling issues, a free meals program, a clothing and furniture distribution center, and a 220-acre retreat center with a camp for underprivileged children and recreation facilities for the community.

HOUSING
Central Union Mission is a high barrier over-night emergency shelter. "High barrier" shelters are shelters that require residents to participate in case management and some house maintenance chores. Central Union Mission accepts residents on a first come, first serve basis. Residents can come in the shelter between the hours of 5pm and 8am for three nights a week. Central Union Mission provides dinner, breakfast, showers, clothing, and social services. Participants are also required to participate in a spiritually based substance abuse program.

MENTAL HEALTH
Central Union Mission also offers an 18-month spiritual-based residential recovery program. Participants must be in good health. They offer Biblical counseling, drug testing, work therapy, literacy and GED classes, and job placement assistance. Phase II of the program requires participants to attend an overnight camp retreat in Montgomery Co., MD. Walk-ins accepted for interviews.
Additional services include:
Guest Services - Emergency shelter services are provided for homeless men every night of the year.
Food Services - Meals and groceries are offered 7 days a week for anyone who walks through the Mission's doors in need.

Community Services - Family and children services, case management services, and special events and celebrations.

Distribution Center - Basic necessities such as clothes, furniture, and food available for pick up .
