 Description: Oxford House is a concept in recovery from drug and alcohol addiction. In its simplest form, an Oxford House describes a democratically run, self-supporting and drug free home. Oxford Houses allows recovering substance abusers to reside and support each other’s recovery through mentoring, encouragement and education. Residents must have income and share expenses in the house. House officials are elected by the respective tenants.

Each House represents a remarkably effective and low cost method of preventing relapse. This was the purpose of the first Oxford House established in 1975, and this purpose is served, day by day, house after house, in each of over 1,200 houses in the United States today. The Eastern House is for women in recovery; a personal interview is required (with respective house representatives) and rent ranges from $50 to $85 per week.
