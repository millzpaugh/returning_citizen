 Description: Unity Health Care Homeless Service Centers have been providing homeless services since 1985. Currently, the program operates 10 homeless medical service sites located in almost every ward of the District of Columbia. Unity provides quality, compassionate and comprehensive primary health care services to homeless individuals and families annually. The services provided by Unity Health Care’s extensive network allow assistance throughout the community including sites located in emergency shelters and homeless service facilities across the city.

Unity Health Care provides people in Washington, DC with quality health care regardless of ability to pay. For those who can pay, a sliding scale based on family size and income is used to determine the appropriate amount. Unity Health Care accepts Medicaid, Medicare, Managed Care Organizations, DC Healthcare Alliance (DC Chartered Health Care Alliance and Unison Alliance), and other private insurance plans. Services offered at the Friendship Place Health Center include the following: Primary medical care for adult men and women, HIV Testing, Psychiatric Services, and TB Screenings.
