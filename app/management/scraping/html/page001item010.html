 Description: Bethesda House is a transitional housing program that serves men over the age of 18 living in Montgomery County who struggle with chronic mental illness. The program focuses on finding permanent housing, securing an income and managing mental health, addiction, legal or health issues. Residents receive case management and counseling; assistance with medication management, meals and access to a laundry room. Referrals must come from Montgomery County Crisis Center (mentally ill). The program meets the 24-hour housing needs of its residents. It also offers case management services helping to secure permanent disability income for residents.
