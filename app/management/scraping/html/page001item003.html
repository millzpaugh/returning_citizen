 Description: The Barbara Chambers Children’s Center (BCCC) offers comprehensive childcare services for children ages 2.6-12 years old, as well as their families. BCCC is committed to serving the low-income families in Washington, DC and the Center operates year round, offering a number of educational opportunities for both children and parents. All programs offer meals and snacks free of charge, and are bilingual in English and Spanish.

PRE-K INCENTIVE:
The Pre-K Incentive Program entails rich academics, structured classroom schedules, and many field trips. The program encourages a love of learning and prepares children and their families for the ever important passage into kindergarten. The Center aims to show children that they can be academically successful despite the many obstacles that arise due to poverty. As with all BCCC programs, each classroom is entirely bilingual in both English and Spanish and operates year round.

BEFORE & AFTER SCHOOL PROGRAM:
This program is offered for school-age children up to age 12. The program operates full-time in the summer months. Children receive academic tutoring from staff and volunteers. Breakfast, snacks, and dinner are provided throughout the year. The opportunities offered allow children to gain valuable exposure to arts, culture, drama, and other “extras” which they would not otherwise have access to. It also reinforces a cornerstone of the program which lies in promoting the development of self-esteem, self-worth, and a sense of belonging and community. This program is bilingual in both English and Spanish and operates year round.

PARENT EDUCATION PROGRAMS:
BCCC offers countless parent education opportunities throughout the year. Parents are able to learn about issues related to parenting including nutrition, discipline, literacy, community resources and more. The program offers cooking demonstrations and food distributions so that parents are able to learn how to cook nutritious produce for their families. Parents receive support, information, and have access to education professionals who are eager to answer their questions. Parents participate free of charge and receive many resources including books, journals, and create their own activities to do at home with their child.

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
Social Security Cards, birth certificates, government-issued ID, proof of DC residency (utility bill), proof of income (Include 3 most recent consecutive paystubs). If you are a student, bring a copy of your school schedule.
