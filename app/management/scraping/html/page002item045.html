 Description: The Church of the Pilgrims believes that God gathers us together as a community of faith in order to send us back out into the world as the Body of Christ, engaging in acts of compassion, and joining others in the struggle for justice and peace. God not only calls us as individuals, but as an entire community, to be part of something bigger than we are. In part of this belief the Church of the Pilgrims runs an Open Table program to feed the homeless and poor once a week.

Open Table has offered lunch to the Dupont Circle area homeless and poor on Sunday afternoons. From 1:00-1:30 PM, we serve a simple meal and provide a sack lunch. In addition to simply giving a meal, we provide our guests with welcome, respect, and a safe space. Open Table is a community effort. We receive funding from Community Council for the Homeless at Friendship Place (CCH/FP) and partner with the Friends Meeting House which serves, cooks, and even provides haircuts once a month. Our volunteer cooks and servers are a blend of Church of the Pilgrims members, including families with young children, and other service-minded individuals in the community.

Nearest Metro Station: Dupont Circle Metro Station. Metro Bus line: D6.
