 Description: The Holy Comforter Community Action Group (CAG) was founded by members of The Holy Comforter-St. Cyprian Catholic Church. This faith-based, community and family-oriented organization provides substance abuse treatment, residential transitional housing, and outpatient programs throughout the District of Columbia. CAG offers three main treatment programs: An Outpatient drug treatment facility, a Men’s residential treatment facility, and a Women’s residential treatment facility with a transitional housing component.

The Women’s Residential Treatment Program offers intensive short term (30 day) residential drug treatment 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Certified addictions counselors, case managers, social workers, and other agency professionals collaborate for a multi-disciplinary approach to treatment planning on a monthly or as needed basis in order to assess progress or further intervention needs. Evidence based treatment modalities include Motivational Enhancement Therapy and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. This facility also offers more trauma focused treatment. The curriculum includes: behavior modification, drug education, relapse prevention, anger management, individual counseling, women’s group, and life skills training. Trained staff also accompanies clients involved with the criminal justice system to court appearances as well as accompany clients with other mandates to their appointments. Specialized sessions for clients who have been dually-diagnosed are also available.

The facility is metro accessible via the Congress Heights station on the green line, as well as, several bus routes including the 92, 94, A4, A6, A8, A42, M9, W2, W4, and W8. Transportation offered in some instances. Call for more details.
