 Description: Partner Arms III is a program developed by the Transitional Housing Corporation (THC). THC provides stable housing and wraparound supportive services to each family in the program that transitions families to permanent housing after a two year period.

Partner Arms III, offers 2-year transitional housing for homeless (13) families. The head of household must agree to cooperate with case management services; contract mental health, and substance abuse educational counseling/therapy. There is no rent payment but participating families must agree to maintain an escrow account throughout the tenancy.

Upon entry into the Partner Arms program, all families participate in a comprehensive psycho-social assessment to determine the immediate and longer-term needs of the participant and their family. Case Managers identify key issues; participants identify and set specific short and long term goals for their two years at Partner Arms. This initial assessment is followed by regular re-assessments every three months.
