 Description: the Newcomer Community Service Center (NCSC) is a minority-based non-profit organization that helps refugees and immigrants from all countries achieve self-sufficiency and become participating members of American society. The Newcomer Community Service Center offers assistance with job placement, counseling, social services, translation, ESL classes, crisis intervention, AIDS education and outreach, immigration services, fingerprinting, photos, and ETS (workforce readiness) testing.

EDUCATION SERVICES

NCSC offers ESL courses at both a beginner level and an Intermediate level. Classes are open to newcomers from all countries and are held Monday through Thursday from 6:30 pm to 8:30 pm. Participants must buy books at a cost of $100. Please call or stop by NCSC's office to register for an ESL class.

EMPLOYMENT & VOCATIONAL TRAINING

NCSC provides a variety of services that help clients obtain and retain employment in order to support themselves and their families. For more information about NCSC's employment services, please contact Shemeles Arega or Eshetu Haile in the DC office at (202) 462-4330.

EMERGENCY FOOD

Staple food is provided by NCSC from 9:00 am to 4:30 pm (Monday-Friday). These services are Metro bus accessible from the 54, S2.

SOCIAL SERVICES

NCSC provides assistance to refugees in resettlement; self-sufficiency; and non-legal immigration services. Assistance is provided, Monday through Friday from 8:30 am to 5:00 pm.
