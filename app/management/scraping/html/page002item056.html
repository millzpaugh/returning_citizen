 Description: The Coalition for the Homeless is a non-profit organization that has served the Washington metropolitan area since 1980. Its mission is to help homeless, at-risk individuals and families return to self-sufficiency and independent living, through a range of residential and social services.

The Webster House is one of the Coalition for the Homeless's transitional rehabilitation homes. Occupants can stay in Webster House for 4-6 months. Occupants must participate in the rehabilitation program. There is no wheelchair access. Persons with mental illness must be stabilized on medication or in group therapy. Webster House provides two (2)meals, employment, housing, financial management counseling, and clothing assistance. There is typically a 3-5 day intake process. Once an application is completed and an interview takes place, the applicant will be placed on a wait-list.
