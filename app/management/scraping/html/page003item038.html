 Description: The District’s Senior Community Service Employment Program (SCSEP) offers subsidized skills training and job placement assistance to disadvantaged District residents ages 55 years and older. SCSEP provides two types of training to non-job ready DC mature job-seekers. Determinations are based upon an initial assessment and the development of an Individual Employment Plan (IEP). The program tailors recruitment, training and employment strategies to help place seniors with host agencies for training and employment in growth industries or with the Department of Employment Services (DOES) for resources to meet employment needs.

All SCSEP participants are considered trainees and are not employees of the DC Department of Employment Services or the federal government. Wages are fixed at the minimum wage level, whichever is higher of the state or federal current minimum wage. The amount is not subject to change based on individual performance. Host Agency assignment and/or training activities may not exceed 20 hours per week. SCSEP participants are compensated for ten federal holidays. No SCSEP participant may work or be paid for working on any federal holiday.

Eligibility Requirements include:
-Be unemployed
-Be a District of Columbia Resident
-Meet the Department of Health and Human Services Poverty Guidelines
-Be 55 years of age or older (additional requirements attached to age requirement)
-No be job-ready due to extended history of unemployment and has not been trained or certified in a trade or skill and has not participated in job-related training or programs.
-Be eligible to work in the US and have the mental and physical capacity to perform work-related activities for at least 20 hours per week.
