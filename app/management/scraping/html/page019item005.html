Tenants Employment Network (TEN) is a 2-year transitional housing program for families (w/children under 18 yrs old). Participants will go through the two phases while being active in the program. The two phase program is broken down as follows: Phase I (6 months) TEN pays full rent, for participants, while residents receive educational, vocational and employment assistance. During phase II (18 months) residents will pay their own rent ($325 to $425) while working or attending school. Program participants who graduate the program are eligible to remain in their TEN apartments without TEN program restrictions.

Program accepts referrals from Virginia Williams Resource Center (202) 526-0071 or a residential substance abuse treatment program.
