 Description: The Family Place helps promote stability and well-being for low income families in Washington DC by offering education, critical resources, and social support. Most of the organizations clients are mothers with young children under the age of five years. All classes and services are offered at no cost to families. The Family Place provides a welcoming, multi-cultural environment, with programs that help meet emergency needs; provide education and training; and offer access to information and other social services. Within the comforts of the Mount Pleasant row house participants not only find access to important services, but also form mutually supportive friendships.

EDUCATION: The Family Place offers a Family Literacy Program where it aims to support families to develop their full potential while delivering developmental appropriate services to children from age zero to five and their parents in the Columbia Heights and Brookland areas. English as a Second Language, Adult Education classes, and Early Childhood Education classes are offered.

SOCIAL SERVICES: The Family Place offers case management, baby furnishings equipment, parenting classes, prenatal classes, domestic violence counseling, and nutrition classes. Childcare provided for children (2 months to 5 years) for participants attending classes. Nearest Metro: Columbia Heights; Metro Bus: S2 & S4

EMERGENCY FOOD/CLOTHING: The Family Place relies greatly on donations of non-perishable foods, adult children's clothing, and diapers to provide to our participants. The Family Place holds a clothing drive once a month, additionally distributing books, toys, car seats, strollers, and furniture if available.
