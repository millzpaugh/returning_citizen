 Description: The mission of the Capital Area Food Bank is to feed those who suffer from hunger in the Washington metro area by acquiring food and distributing it through its network of partner agencies; and educating, empowering and enlightening the community about the issues of hunger and nutrition.

FOOD REFERRALS
The Capital Area Food Bank’s Hunger Lifeline is an emergency food assistance referral program for residents in the Washington metro region that connects families and individuals seeking immediate aid with partner agencies.

FOOD DISTRIBUTION
The Capital Area Food Bank, through a network of over 700 partner agencies, efficiently sources and distributes 30 million pounds of food annually, half of which is fresh produce, to residents struggling with hunger in the Washington metro region.
