 Description: Voices for Second Chance (VSC) is an essential safety net for offenders and their families, who are most likely to be among the most marginalized members of our society. VSC’s goal is to reduce the recidivism rate in the District by providing assistance to some of the Nation’s neediest residents, as returning citizens who are given services pre-and-post release from incarceration make a successful transition to become positive contributing citizens to our economy and community. VSC is committed to a long history of providing basic human services to meet basic human needs.

Services include the following: First Responder Services, Drop-in/Re-Entry, Casework, Food Stamps, Family Support and VSC Bridges the Gap.

First Responders Services is an intake program that provides, at the time of incarceration, a variety of basic functions like calling family member(s), picking up property that was left at a shelter or with the police, etc. Such casework services continue through the incarceration period.

The VSC Drop-In Program welcomes newly released inmates daily who visit our office for basic human needs. Staff empowers clients to take control of their new release by providing tools to support reentry back into the community from CDF and CTF providing assistance with: Identification Retrieval, Resume Preparation, Employment Search, Education & Training Prospects, Transportation Support, Housing and Emergency Shelter, and Clothing Needs.

In the VSC Drop-In Program clients are offered basic human needs such as telephone calls to loved ones and significant others, use of the internet, bus tokens to get home, funds for a birth certificate or police clearance to gain identification, a snack, toiletries (soap/toothbrush/toothpaste), or a haircut certificate. VSC staff also coordinates drug, alcohol, anger management, batterers' intervention, money management, and other life skills educational sessions.

Through the VSC’s Bridge the Gap program services offered include: Family Connection: Bedtime Stories for Children, Birth Certificates/Social Security cards, Voter registration, income tax preparation, police clearances, and temporary mailing program.
