 Description: The Jacob Burns Community Legal Clinics (George Washington Law School) provides services Civil and Family Litigation; Federal, Criminal and Appellate services; Health Rights Law; Immigration; and Public Justice Advocacy.

Advocacy services for Civil and Family Litigation include representation in family law, divorce, custody, child support and limited small claims.

Federal, Criminal and Appellate Litigation services include direct representation in proceedings following criminal convictions. Health Rights Law services include direct advocacy for securing health benefits through Medicaid, HMO or other managed care benefits; assistance with health insurance disputes and collection agencies.

Immigration Law services include direct assistance in helping aliens obtain eligible public benefits and deportation prevention.

Public Justice Advocacy services include class and individual representation in employment termination, wage and hour disputes, public education and injured workers.

In order to be eligible for services through this law clinic all potential clients must meet the indigent income guidelines, if applicable.
