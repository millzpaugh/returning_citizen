 Description: Covenant House Washington is the largest provider serving homeless, runaway and at-risk young people between the ages of 18 and 24. Programming offered includes social services, employment and vocational training, and education services.

SOCIAL SERVICES:
Case management is an essential component of our residential and Workforce Readiness Education and Training (WRET) programming. Young people work with case managers to identify their strengths and challenges and then develop and implement individual service plans. When Covenant House Washington does not offer a service that a particular young person wants or needs, the case manager can draw upon an extremely comprehensive list of external service providers with whom we have partnered in the past.

EMPLOYMENT & VOCATIONAL TRAINING:
Through the job training and readiness programs, homeless teens can gain skills in a specific vocation and about job hunting and the professional world. Residents are given interview clothes, participate in mock interviews to coach them on how to land a job, help with résumé preparation, and skills to cultivate relationships with local employers for future job placement opportunities. Job training programs include certification courses in: Culinary arts; Desktop publishing; Medical assistant professions; Public safety; Computer skills; Woodworking and more.

EDUCATION:
The Covenant House provides education and work-readiness training. Young people can connect with Adult Basic Education (ABE) and General Education Development (GED) services through the Work Readiness Education and Training (WRET) Center. For more information regarding WRET contact Robert Johns, WRET coordinator, at rjohns@chdc.org.
