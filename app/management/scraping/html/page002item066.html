Description: Community Family Life Services (CFLS) offers provides a range of programs and services that help clients address the immediate and long term factors resulting from, or contributing to, poverty and homelessness. Services are offered to provide stable housing, support youth achievement, mentors young people in foster care, care for people with HIV/AIDS, mentors families, meets emergency needs assist seniors and spreads good will. SOCIAL SERVICES CFLS provides case management for individuals and families that are infected with HIV/AIDS. Case managers help locate housing opportunities, manage treatment and medical care adherence, assist with client education and work with other programs in the District to facilitate a healthy transition to a state of self-sufficiency. HOUSING Through two residential programs Trinity Arms and Milestone Place, CFLS provides individual adults and families with children a safe, secure, and supportive place to live while they work or receive training and services needed to rebuild successful lives. EDUCATION The CFLS after-school program is hosted at Leckie Elementary School and serves youth ages 7-13 years old with a focus on academic enrichment and positive development. Students benefit from high quality academic tutoring and mentoring, as well as art and cultural enrichment programs. EMERGENCY FOOD/CLOTHING CFLS provides emergency food and clothing to person in need. Food bags are distributed every Tuesday from 12:00 noon to 1:00 pm, and at other times with a referral notice. Clothing is distributed from 10:00 am to 12:00 noon to women on Wednesday and Thursdays and to men on Fridays. Children’s clothing is distributed at either time. For additional information call (202) 347-0511 x346. CFLS also coordinates food delivery and matches services with needs for homebound Seniors in the District. PARENTING The CFLS Parent Resource & Oasis Center (ROC) provides parent, guardians and caregivers with the knowledge and skills needed to cope with and address issues affecting their ability to nurture and contribute to their family, family environment, and their children’s sense of safety, structure, and success. Weekly Parent ROC meetings are held at both Leckie Elementary School and at CFLS.
