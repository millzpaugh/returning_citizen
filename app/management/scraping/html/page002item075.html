 Description: The Comprehensive Psychiatric Emergency Program (CPEP) operates twenty-four hours a day, seven days a week. It provides emergency psychiatric services, mobile crisis services and extended observation beds for individuals experiencing a psychiatric emergency and are 18 years of age and older in homes and on the street throughout the city who may be unwilling or unable to travel to receive mental health services. Mobile crisis services teams can be activated by contacting the Access Helpline (1-888-793-4357) and are available from 9:00 am – 1:00 am daily. These teams provide crisis stabilization including dispensing medication and assessing the need for voluntary or involuntary hospitalizations, as well as, linkage to other services like substance abuse detoxification and treatment. The teams also work with family members and the community based mental health provider to assist with follow up.
