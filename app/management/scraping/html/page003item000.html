 Description: Damien Ministries offers services to DC residents who are HIV positive. Services provided are in the form of emergency food, housing, and social services.

SOCIAL SERVICES:
There are two requirements to receive a Damien Ministries case manager. First, potential clients must test positive for HIV. Secondly, the potential client must be a Washington, DC resident. Clients are often referred to us through other service organizations, but phone calls from potential clients are also accepted.

CASE MANAGER QUALIFICATIONS:
A client should be referred through a social worker, counselor, or other case manager call Damien Ministries at (202)526-3020 and dial extension 15, 16, or 19. If already a food bank client, you may set up an appointment around your scheduled food bank appointment Visit Damien Ministries Monday through Friday, 10:00am to 6:00pm

FOOD BANK:
The Damien Ministries Food Bank steps in to help so clients don't have to choose between medicine or food. Provisions of two to four bags of groceries per month are given to clients in need, with enough food supplements to last up to 14 days.

ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS:
In order to qualify for food bank services, clients must be HIV-positive and be able to show financial need. Clients are required to provide a letter of referral, usually from a case manager, explaining the financial need, as well as proof of HIV status from either a case manager or medical professional. It is recommended that case managers call in advance and speak with the Food Bank Manager to confirm food bank hours and availability.

Additional services are offered through Damien Ministries Faith Initiatives, which include Spiritual Retreats, the Youth Safe Space Project, and Faith Advocacy Intervention: Targeting HIV/AIDS (F.A.I.T.H).
