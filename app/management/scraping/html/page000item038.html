 Description: Andromeda’s mission is to provide access to quality healthcare under the premise that all humans are inherently of equal worth but have cultural and linguistic differences that must be considered. This philosophy reflects a high level of community health care that has been validated by over three decades of direct service to minorities and under-served populations.

Andromeda Transcultural Health provides access to quality healthcare to minorities and under-served populations, namely those with cultural and linguistic differences. They provide prevention and treatment of HIV/AIDS, HIV testing, primary care, case management, substance abuse counseling, mental health counseling focusing on trauma, anger management, domestic violence, grief and loss, and referral for housing and hospitalization. These services are free to DC residents. Appointments for primary care can be made with the Physician’s Assistant on Mondays and Wednesdays from 9:00 am – 5:00 pm.

Andromeda Transcultural Health also provides individual and group counseling for the dually diagnosed; children’s program with emphasis on depression and attention deficit disorders (children seen on Saturdays only); Latino Women’s group every Thursday evening (free and conducted in Spanish); adult psychiatric treatment with access to hospitalization at Washington Hospital Center and Howard University Hospital. Andromeda Transcultural Health also runs a mobile outreach vehicle (Project Orion) that provides HIV testing, primary care, case management, referral and education services. Accepts public funding, self-payment ($50 flat fee per visit), Medicaid, Charter Health, some Private insurance (call in advance to see if they accept your insurance). Spanish and French speaking staff available. Day program currently not being offered.
