 Description: St. Ann's currently operates three residential programs and a community day care center. The center's programs include: a Residential Children's Program, Teen-Mother Baby Program, Child Care Center and the Faith House.

RESIDENTIAL CHILDREN'S PROGRAM: St. Ann's provides emergency placement and short-term residential care for infants and young children, ages 2 days through 12 years, who have been abused, neglected, or abandoned. Our goal for each child is to offer a caring, comfortable, and reassuring environment and to provide services based on a complete assessment of the child's physical, emotional, developmental, and educational needs.

TEEN MOTHER-BABY PROGRAM: The program offers residential care for pregnant adolescents and young mothers and their babies. Extensive services include an accredited high school, medical care, parenting classes, life skills training, day care, individual and family counseling, and social and cultural activities.

CHILD CARE CENTER: St. Ann’s Child Care Center provides affordable, developmental day care for infants and preschool children in the community and in residence, including children with special needs, and children from low-income, single parent families and from families in crisis.

FAITH HOUSE: At Faith House, transitional apartment housing is available for 8 young mothers (18–25 years old) and their children. Residents work toward economic self-sufficiency and stable family life with the help of job placement, day care, parenting classes, and other support.
