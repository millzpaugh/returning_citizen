 Description: The Community Council for the Homeless at Friendship Place is the neighborhood's answer to the problem of homelessness. The new Community Council for the Homeless at Friendship Place offers street outreach, a drop-in center, free medical and psychiatric care, supportive services, and case management for those in the small congregation shelters and permanent housing program.

TRANSITIONAL HOUSING
With the ongoing case management that we offer, the residents of our housing programs can recover from the fear, loneliness and illness they experienced on the streets, and can reach their fullest potential for self-sufficiency and integration into the community. The case managers work with them to repair broken family relationships, get clean and sober, tend to medical and psychiatric issues, learn or re-learn basic life skills, put their finances in order, pursue educational opportunities, and find work or enter job training programs.

SOCIAL SERVICES
Through Friendship Place’s Drop-in Welcome Center homeless individuals have access to the following services and many of the services are free: Case Management, Telephone and Voice Mail Services, Medical and Psychiatric Care, Employment Services, Mail Services, Computer Lab, and Food and Drink. All are welcome to utilize the services offered at the Welcome Center.
