 Description: House of Ruth helps women, children and families in greatest need and with very limited resources to build safe, stable lives and achieve their highest potential. At House of Ruth, women, children and families heal from lifetimes of traumatic abuse. Programs and services are focused on creating and sustaining positive change for the women, children and families we serve.

DOMESTIC VIOLENCE SUPPORT CENTER
The Domestic Violence Support Center offers free psychotherapy to women who are affected by domestic violence. The women served at the Support Center are from all walks of life and diverse racial and socio-economic backgrounds. Services are offered in individual psychotherapy and case management services are also provided to assist women in their transition.

INDIVIDUAL PSYCHOTHERAPY
Individual psychotherapy helps the women establish emotional and physical safety, reduce anxiety, build cognitive skills, establish goals for future, and experience feelings without impulsively acting on them. The therapists also connect the women with other resources in the community

CASE MANAGEMENT SERVICES
Case management services provide an individualized plan, developed with each woman, focusing on her immediate safety, her mental health and addressing related issues. The therapists help women gain access to critical resources for housing, employment, education and training, child care, legal issues, budgeting and safety planning. In addition, the Support Center staff reaches out to many other organizations that may come in contact with people who are dealing with domestic violence to acquaint them with the supports and services available at House of Ruth.
