 Description: Catholic Charities is the social ministry outreach of the Archdiocese of Washington. Motivated by the Gospel message of Jesus Christ, and guided by Catholic social and moral teaching, Catholic Charities strengthens the lives of all in need by giving help that empowers and hope that lasts. To this end the organization affirms and supports the dignity of all human life, strengthens families and serves the poor and most vulnerable.

The New York Avenue Emergency Shelter is an emergency shelter for men over the age of 18. The program offers a hot meal, a shower, a place to stay off of the streets, access to a case manager and a work program to give clients experience in the work environment. The shelter also runs a clothing store for free clothing to clients. Shuttle service is offered to several locations in downtown DC. Intake begins at 7:00 pm each night and the shelter closes at 7:00 am the next morning.

This is a year-round shelter and housing assistance center with comprehensive medical care, substance abuse, vocational, and mental health services.

This shelter serves as a hypothermia shelter during the winter months between October and March when temperatures drop to deadly lows, which means the shelter is a low barrier shelter. A low barrier shelter does not require residents to accept services as a condition of staying at the shelter.
