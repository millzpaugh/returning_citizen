 Description: The Institute for African Man Development provides a wide range of social services, to increase positive mental health of young men through therapeutic self-examination; increase life skills that help young men negotiate life more effectively; promote healthier relationships through social-economical development; assist young men in understanding their roles as fathers and increase their presence in their children’s lives; equip those who work with this population, wit effective intervention strategies; establish an information Clearinghouse for African-American men and boys; and link young men to much needed community resources.

The Institute for African Man Development currently offers the following services on a contractual basis:

Individual Therapy; Family Therapy; Group Therapy; Support Groups; Case Management; Parenting/Fatherhood Training; Life Management Skills Training; Character Education; Home-Based Services; Mentoring; and Enrichment Retreats.

The Training Institute currently offers training's to service providers across the country using curriculum we have developed:

Effective Clinical Interventions with African-American Men and Boys; Manhood Development; The Emancipated Boy; Preparing Men and Boys for Male/Female Relationships; Racism and Sexism; African-American Men and Addictive Behavior; Effective Fathering Strategies.
