 Description: Lutheran Social Services has four locations that provide services in adoption, foster care, disaster preparation and response, and refugee/immigrant services. The main office provides services in adoption for the District of Columbia and Maryland, foster care, camps and disaster preparation and response services.

ADOPTION SERVICES The adoption program has two main components: home study and post-placement visits for domestic and international adoptions. Trained professionals will help you navigate every step of the adoption process and conduct your home study and post-placement visits.

FOSTER CARE PROGRAM
Working in partnership with the District of Columbia Child and Family Services Administration CFSA), Lutheran Social Services recruits and trains adults locally to become foster parents for neglected or abused children living in the District of Columbia. For information regarding unaccompanied refugee minor care please see website.

CAMP PROGRAMS
Camp programs are offered for kids aged 7-13, teens aged 14-21 and for entire families.

KID CAMP
Camp Kids Haven is a week-long get-away on the campus of the Mar-Lu Ridge Camp and Conference Center in Jefferson, MD. Campers get to swim, play games, sleep in sleeping bags, do arts and crafts projects, and go on adventures in the woods.

TEEN CAMP
Teen Haven Retreats is a series of three weekend retreats held throughout the year, each at a different location. The teen retreats aim to provide youth with life skills, strong role models, and opportunities for creative self-expression as they plan for their future as young adults. Mentoring and special day activities are also available to teens between retreats.

FAMILY CAMP
Family Mini-Camp, is an overnight retreat for parents/caregivers and children to enjoy a taste of camp together. Parenting support classes and activities also take place several times annually to help adults as they deal with the particular challenges of raising children when HIV is "in the family."

DISASTER PREPARATION AND RESPONSE
Services are offered in both disaster preparation and response. The services provided are both immediate and far reaching (such as shelter, food and medical care). Relocation services and employment programs are also offered to help victims get back on their feet.
