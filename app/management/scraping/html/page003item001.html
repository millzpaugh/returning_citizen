 Description: The Daniel Alexander Payne Reclamation Program (DAPRP) was established to combat recidivism by focusing on the needs of ex-offenders returning from prison to the Washington metropolitan area. The purpose of the project is to help direct and support ex-offenders’ successful transition from prison into the community. In the past the program has been able to work with a network of twenty (20) local organizations and churches who partnered or collaborated with DAPRP to provide services focusing on the multiple needs of ex-offenders attempting to re-enter the local community, in particular (1) health, (2) literacy, (3) housing, (4) employment, and (5) clothing. These critical service providers made their resources available to the participating DAPRP clientele. Approximately 70 ex-offenders participated in the program. One-on-one mentoring activities, provided by 70 mentors from Metro-area churches, were also available to the DAPRP clientele.
