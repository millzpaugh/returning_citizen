 Description: Partner Arms II is a program developed by the Transitional Housing Corporation (THC). THC provides stable housing and wraparound supportive services to each family in the program that transitions families to permanent housing after a two year period.

Partner Arms II offers a 2-year transitional housing for 14 homeless families. Participants must be drug-free for at least 6 months. Partner Arms II provides job placement assistance, skills training, child advocacy counseling, and substance abuse counseling. They also accept referrals from most social service providers. No walk-ins accepted.

Upon entry into the Partner Arms program, all families participate in a comprehensive psycho-social assessment to determine the immediate and longer-term needs of the participant and their family. Case Managers identify key issues; participants identify and set specific short and long term goals for their two years at Partner Arms. This initial assessment is followed by regular re-assessments every three months.
