 Description: The Executive Addiction Disease Program (EADP) provides evaluation, education, and outpatient treatment services for clients struggling with alcohol and drug problems. EADP offers solutions for individuals and families working to address the issue of chemical dependence in their lives. EADP helps individuals working from the fragmented paradigm of chemical dependency to develop the tools needed to have a more integrated and prioritized view of problems and their solutions. EADP touches the community with resources through the Outpatient program, Drug and Alcohol Education program and the Young Adult program.

Outpatient Program The focus of group work is abstinence and relapse prevention, engaging in a recovery fellowship and building support systems, and understanding alcoholism and addiction in the context of the family. Each group provides a supportive and confidential space for members to make the needed changes in their lives to develop a lasting recovery from alcohol and drug dependence. Additionally, individual and family counseling services are provided to our clients and their families as needed.

DRUGN AND ALCOHOL EDUCATION PROGRAM:
EADP provides an education component for individuals who have been charged with driving while under the influence and other alcohol/drug related offenses. Topics such as the scope of the drinking and driving problem in the United States, the hazards, consequences, and prevention of drinking and driving, other drugs of abuse, synergistic effects, and their hazards are a few of the topics covered during these classes.

YOUNG ADULT PROGRAM:
At EADP, we understand the unique challenges facing young adults and their families seeking treatment for alcohol and chemical dependency. Our Young Adult Program is aimed at helping clients aged 18 to 30 in sharing the challenges and successes of early recovery. Our goal at EADP is to help our clients develop the necessary tools for a long term recovery in an environment of encouragement and accountability. With attention given to family dynamics, social pressure, and co-occurring disorders, we engage our clients in a process of self-exploration while they plan the next phase of their life, with recovery as the necessary foundation.
