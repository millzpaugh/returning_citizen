 Description: Howard University School of Law offers law clinic services in criminal justice, alternative dispute resolution, civil rights and fair housing.

The Criminal Justice Clinic is operated by 3rd year law students under the licensed supervision of an experienced attorney. Representation services are provided for misdemeanor adults and juveniles charged in the DC Superior Court (as assigned by the Court).

The Alternative Dispute Resolution Clinic is where law students mediate disputes of small claims, domestic and property issues (cases assigned by Court).

Through the Civil Rights Clinic clients may receive trial and appellate representation in federal and state courts. Cases taken under advisement are social justice matters related to: employment and housing; voting rights; police brutality; unconstitutional prison conditions; habeas corpus; unfair barriers to the courts. Referrals are accepted by court appointments, private referrals, write-in requests and scheduled intake interviews.

Fair Housing Clinic (students provide administrative hearing representation, advocacy, research support and training in housing discrimination matters).
