 Description: The Latin American Youth Center (LAYC) offers an intensive outpatient drug abuse counseling for ages 14 to 21 years with case management and aftercare. The outpatient program helps youth and families reach improved levels of cognitive and emotional functioning and behavioral change. Program emphasizes positive alternatives to drug use. Direct referrals and walk-ins accepted and the program is an APRA Choice Voucher provider. LAYC also offers resource and development programs for at-risk youths (12 to 21 yrs) and their families. LAYC programs include: (1) Diversion Program- Counseling to support and strengthen youth and families to help achieve personal, educational and career aspirations and maintain healthy, self-sufficient lifestyles. (2) Host Homes-Temporary shelter for up to two weeks for homeless and runaway female youth in a culturally sensitive setting. (3) Family Strengthening- 13-session parent education that addresses violence prevention, including family’s roles and communication anger management. (4) Focus on Kids- 12-week curriculum focused on HIV and substance abuse prevention ages 10-17. Incorporates decision-making skills, values clarification, communication styles, and information about risky behavior voucher program for substance abuse treatment. (5) Foster Care- Services to children 0 - 18 who have been temporarily removed from their biological homes as a result of abuse or neglect. (6) Street Outreach Program- Emergency shelter, education and prevention services to runaway youth. (7) Substance Abuse- Outpatient individual, group and family counseling; drug testing. (8) Transitional Living Program- Supervised residential facility for 10 males (17–21), for up to 18 months. Youths are assisted towards independent living.
