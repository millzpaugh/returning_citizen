 Eligibility Criteria: Must be under the supervision of Court Services and Offender Supervision Agency (CSOA) for referral.
 Description: VOTEE staff utilizes educational and vocational assessment tools to assess the needs of offenders who are referred to VOTEE Centers. Referrals for educational, basic technology and employability training are under the supervision of the Court Services and Offender Supervision Agency (CSOSA), with no age restrictions for participants.

Participants will undergo occupational and educational assessments and will receive an Individual Development Plan (IDP) and/or an Individual Education Plan (IEP) prepared by CSOSA staff. Learning Lab Specialists assist offenders in improving their educational level, facilitate life skills preparation and administer the educational and vocational assessments. In addition, the unit provides Information Systems Technology training (IST). The educational services provided for participants include: Adult Basic Education (ABE), General Equivalency Diploma prep (GED), English as a Second Language (ESL), and core/life skills.

Vocational Development Specialists assist offenders in preparing for employment opportunities and/or refers offenders to vocational programs based on vocational assessments and the offender’s interest, skills and abilities. The employment services provided through VOTEE include: employability assessments for job readiness; customer service skills, communication skills, interpersonal skills, skills identification, resume writing, interviewing skills, problem solving, ethics, drug free workplace, sexual harassment, and the keys to keeping a job.
