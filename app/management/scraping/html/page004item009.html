 Description: The Elizabeth House operates under Borromeo House, Inc. (BHI) and through BHI the Elizabeth House is able to offer services through a residential program, which includes social services and the Infant Care Supply Center. The transitional housing is for homeless adolescent mothers (ages 16 to 21) with one child or pregnant with first child. Program participants will be provided with supervised living arrangements that require participation in program activities and supportive services; parenting skills training; finance and budgeting seminars; mental health counseling; vocational and educational assessment/aid; and case management. DC residents considered.

RESIDENTIAL PROGRAM:
BHI’s signature program empowers young single mothers to create a self-sustaining future through education, counseling and support, while residing in our safe home. BHI closely monitors the infants of young mothers to assure the children are parented responsibly, receive appropriate medical care, and meet individual developmental benchmarks.

INFANT CARE SUPPLY CENTER:
The Infant Care Supply Center provides applicants, who are not eligible for the Residential program with infant supplies which may include diapers, car seats, clothing, and other infant care supplies each month, free of cost.
