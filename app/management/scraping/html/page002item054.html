 Description: The Coalition for the Homeless is a non-profit organization that has served the Washington metropolitan area since 1980. Its mission is to help homeless, at-risk individuals and families return to self-sufficiency and independent living, through a range of residential and social services.

Spring Road Family Apartment Program is part of the Coalition for the Homeless, Family Services program. The program provides shelter and programs that help homeless families regain stability in their lives and address the root cause of poverty, dysfunction, and homelessness. The program focuses on substance abuse, mental health, employment and training, housing issues, and the development and educational needs of the children.

During a family’s stay at Spring Road, parents are able to work toward self-sufficiency in a safe, regimented atmosphere. Families prepare their own meals and maintain their apartments, while participating in self-empowering activities. Families receive comprehensive services, which will enable them to become economically self-sufficient. The staff offers support to residents through substance abuse counseling, mental health, and housing and employment services.

Spring Road Family Apartments provides 28 apartment units dedicated to serving families who have temporarily become homeless. All families are referred by the Virginia Williams Family Resource Center, which is the central intake office in the District for families requesting emergency housing.
