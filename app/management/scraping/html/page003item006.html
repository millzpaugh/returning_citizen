 Description: DC Central Kitchen (DCCK) offers a 14-week basic food service training course through their Culinary Job Training Program. The goal of the program is to prepare, unemployed, underemployed, previously incarcerated persons and homeless persons (preferably in transitional housing) for careers in food service. Participants in the program will cover all facets of work in a professional kitchen. The program includes “hands-on” training and local chefs also volunteer once a week to teach specific skills that include: knife skills, fruit, vegetables & fresh herbs, stocks & sauces, meat & seafood cookery, cooking techniques & methods, grains a& pastas, garde manger and special events.

ELIGIBILITY:
To be eligible for the program persons must be, 18 yrs +, available to attend class from 8:30 am to 4:00 pm, maintain a status of 120 days drug free, ability to lift 50 lbs, and be able to stand minimum 4 hrs. The training cycles are offered 4 times per year (Jan., April, July and Oct.) and classes only enroll 25 students per class cycle. Upon completion of the program, graduates continue to work with DCCK’s training and employment staff. Graduates are assisted in an intense job search to obtain full-time employment at local restaurants, hotels, caterers and other hospitality businesses.
