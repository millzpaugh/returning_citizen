 Description: Progress Place is a facility owned by Montgomery County which has two primary programs, Shepherd's Table and Community Vision, offering services to the homeless in the Silver Spring Area. Community Vision is a day program that provides daily services critical to the safety and well being of homeless men and women in Montgomery County. While Shepherd's Table provides services that include meals, a Clothes Closet, Eye Clinic and a Resource Center.

Meals The dinner meal service is at the heart of the mission of Shepherd's Table and the program serves brunch on Saturday and Sunday and evening meals daily. Hours of Operation: Brunch Meal Served Saturday and Sunday 10:30 am - 11:15 am Evening Meal Served Daily 6:00 pm – 7:30 pm

EYE CLINIC The Eye Clinic was provides vision services for uninsured persons, which include vision testing, glaucoma screening, and prescription eyeglass service. In order to be eligible to receive free eye care the following criteria must be met: Must be an adult 18 years old or older; Must be a resident of Montgomery County; Household income must be below 175% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines; Must not have medical insurance that covers vision care; and must be referred by an authorized Montgomery County government or private nonprofit agency. The Eye Clinic is open Tuesdays 10:00 a.m. – 2:00 p.m.

CLOTHES CLOSET The Clothes Closet is open Wednesdays 10:00 a.m. – 11:30 a.m. The program is able to provide clean, gently-used clothing for all seasons to guests. Clients can select from clothing, books for adults and children, children’s toys, handbags and other accessories. Selection may be limited based on supply. The program also offers shower facilities, new underwear and socks, clean towels, deodorant, and other toiletries.

RESOURCE CENTER The Resource Center offers services helps to improve the daily lives of clients and dinner guest by offering: access to personal mail and telephone services, token assistance to reach shelters, toiletries, blankets, over the counter medication(s), and referrals for shelter services.

The Center also offers basic case management services, information and referral, advocacy, bi-lingual services, immigration assistance, prescription assistance and crisis intervention. The Center coordinates the on-site services of: Legal Aid lawyers, Department of Veterans Affairs outreach workers, barbers, and tax preparers.

The Resource Center staff works in coordination with the staff from Community Vision, a program in the same building, to best serve the needs of the men and women utilizing the services of both programs. Staff also works closely with other providers in the County and the greater DC area, including Montgomery County Coalition for the Homeless.
