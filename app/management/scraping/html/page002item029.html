 Description: The Center for Child Protection and Family Support(CCPFS) is a community-based nonprofit social service and advocacy agency that focuses on child protection and safety. The Center is nationally and international known for delivery of services, training & technical assistance to providers, and programs that prevent violence. Its services impact children and families throughout the District of Columbia and nationally for the prevention of child abuse. The Center offers a Trauma Informed Positive Parenting program for parents who are survivors of domestic violence and women reentering the community after incarcerations. Each TIPP training session is two-hours in length, and consists of small groups of participants. A certificate of completion is provided to each parent/caregiver that completes the program. The Center offers parent education and skill-building programs to families as well.

The parent education program is 14 weeks long and participants may incur a minimal fee. In addition The Center provides support groups for care givers, anger management classes and domestic violence classes. The Center also offers professional training programs designed to empower, educate and inform child welfare professionals with up-to-the minute information about preventing, identifying and reporting child abuse and neglect. Some fees may apply. However, the majority of services are publicly funded. Contact The Center for more information.
