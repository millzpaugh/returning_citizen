 Description: HIPS (Helping Individual Prostitutes Survive) assist female, male, and transgender individuals engaging in sex work in Washington, DC in leading healthy lives. Using a harm reduction model, HIPS’ programs strive to address the impact that HIV/AIDS, sexually transmitted infections, discrimination, poverty, violence and drug use have on the lives of individuals engaging in sex work. Programs offered include:

MOBILE OUTREACH/EDUCATION
HIPS provides safer sex materials, syringe access, counseling, and support to street-based workers Thursday, Friday and Saturday nights from 11pm to 5 am. Outreach includes: free HIV Testing and Counseling, Safer sex supplies and education, Syringe exchange, Bad date sheets, Referrals to HIPS and community resources, and Emotional support for people on the stroll.

PEER EDUCATION HIPS trains former or current sex workers to educate their peers on safety and HIV. The Peer Educators extend HIPS programs into areas traditional health programs can’t reach.

CLIENT ADVOCACY HIPS provides basic services, such as helping to obtain an ID card or finding emergency shelter, clothing closet, free HIV/AIDS testing and counseling (also one on one coaching and counseling), referrals for services and crisis intervention services.

COMMUNITY EDUCATION
HIPS trains medical and social service providers to appropriately address the needs of sex workers and address barriers sex workers face in accessing services.
