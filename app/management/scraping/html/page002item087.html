The Crisis Center provides 44 beds for immediate, short-term emergency shelter to homeless single and parenting young people, who lack a safe place to lay their heads at night. Young people are provided with an individual case plan, enrolled in job preparedness and/or job training programs, provided with on-site GED preparedness classes and are provided with a wardrobe of work-appropriate clothing to being their job search.

The Rights of Passage (ROP) is an innovative transitional living program that addresses the long-term needs of at-risk kids who first come in crisis. Once accepted into Rights of Passage from the Crisis Care program, residents live in semi-independence at Covenant House for up to 18 months. Youth have access to services that include: skill and education assessments, employment and educational assistance, individual planning and goal-setting, vocational assistance (interviewing skills, proper dress code, job retention, and employer/employee interactions), job readiness skills and employment support, independent living skills (workshop topics: paying rent, maintaining a checking account, and setting short/long-term goals), financial development and budget management, full-service child development center, after-care to ensure stability and continued pursuit of goals and coordination and referral to community providers.

Eligibility requires young people to be between the ages of 18 and 24 years, be a homeless single or parenting youth; maintain employment, as well as, enroll in Covenant House Washington’s employment and educational assistance programs.
