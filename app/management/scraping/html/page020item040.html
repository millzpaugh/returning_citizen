 Description: Us Helping Us, People Into Living, Inc. provides an array of individual-level, group-level and community-level programs and services targeting the African-American community, particularly Black gay and same-gender-loving men. All of our programs and services are free depending on eligibility. INDIVIDUAL LEVEL SERVICES:
HIV Counseling, Testing and Referral Services: Free HIV Test is a service that can be utilized by everyone. You have the option of anonymous or confidential testing. No blood. No needles. Results in 20 minutes. Trained staff is available weekdays, and Saturdays by appointment.

CHLAMYDIA AND GONORRHEA SCREENINGS:
In addition to HIV testing services, the program offers urine screenings, as well as oral and anal swabs for Chlamydia and Gonorrhea.

CASE MANAGEMENT SERVICES:
The program has case managers help individuals to secure entitlements and assistance with housing, emergency financial aid, food, medical or dental care. To qualify for case management, a person must be HIV-positive, reside in the District of Columbia and not have an income 300% above the poverty level.

TREATMENT ADHERENCE:
The Treatment Adherence Services provide counseling, peer education, or a mentor to help you stay on track with HIV medication.

INDIVIDUAL COUNSELING:
The program provides individual counseling and psychotherapy for individuals, regardless of their HIV status. If you think counseling or psychotherapy would help you with personal issues or concerns, licensed therapist are available. The facility is a reliable, private, non-judgmental space. All clients will be given the time needed to ask questions, face your fears, and discover who you are or want to be.

GROUP LEGAL PROGRAMS:
Group level programs include, weekly support groups and discussion groups and Narcotics Anonymous (NA) 12-Step meeting. While the Community level programs include: Street and Venue Outreach, Popular Opinion Leader (POL), D-Up!, and Be-The Generation Project. For more details on group and community level programs see website.
