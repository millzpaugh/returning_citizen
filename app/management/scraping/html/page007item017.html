 Description: The Holy Comforter Community Action Group (CAG) was founded by members of The Holy Comforter-St. Cyprian Catholic Church. This faith-based, community and family-oriented organization provides substance abuse treatment, residential transitional housing, and outpatient programs throughout the District of Columbia. CAG offers three main treatment programs: An Outpatient drug treatment facility, a Men’s residential treatment facility, and a Women’s residential treatment facility with a transitional housing component.

The Adult Outpatient and Intensive Outpatient Treatment Program offers individual therapy, Men’s Group Therapy, Women’s Group Therapy, and Co-ed Group Therapy through evidence based treatment models such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy and Motivational Enhancement Therapy. Access To Recovery (ATR) services include: recovery support evaluation, care coordination, spiritual support, recovery mentoring / coaching, education services, HIV/AIDS education, life skills, parenting services, family / marital services, childcare, and transportation. This program is open to DC residents only and referrals for services are accepted from APRA, PSA, and CSOSA – no walk-ins. This program is publicly funded through APRA’s Access to Recovery Voucher program.

This facility is metro accessible and can be accessed via the Potomac Avenue metro station on the Blue / Orange line, as well as several bus routes including the 32, 36, 90, 96, All, A42, B2, D6, K11, M6, P1, and V8 to name a few.
