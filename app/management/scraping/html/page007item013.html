 Description: Hillcrest Children’s Center (HCC) is a non-profit, freestanding outpatient mental health facility located in the District of Columbia. HCC provides a full range of services in a variety of settings designed to meet the psychological needs of children, adolescents, and families who are confronted with emotional issues in today’s society. HCC’s team of professionals is committed to providing services that promote the well-being of children and families to ensure that their mental health needs are met within their homes, school, and community environments.

HCC is certified by the DC Department of Mental Health as a Core Service Agency (CSA) and provides outpatient mental health services primarily to child and their families, but also accepts adult mental health clients as well through the Access Helpline. Contact the Access Helpline 1-877-793-4357 to inquire about adult enrollment. HCC accepts APRA vouchers, Medicaid, Chartered Health, Healthright, and Unison.

SERVICES:
Individual, Group, and Family Therapy: Counseling for individuals, groups and families to resolve symptoms of behavioral, emotional, social, and cognitive disorders.

Psychiatric Evaluation: An intensive psychiatric and psycho-social evaluation of the child’s emotional and behavioral problems.

Parenting Education: Individuals and groups learn and practice skills essential to successful child rearing and healthy family relationships.

Anger and Stress Management: Individual and group counseling is offered to youths whose anger is out of control and is contributing to problems at school, work, and in their personal relationships.

Youth Substance Abuse Treatment: Turning Point is a 90-day intensive outpatient program for youths and families. Youths are required to attend group sessions and undergo individual counseling. Case management services engage and support the family.

Youth Substance Abuse Prevention: Project Success is a science-based program conducted in the schools and natural group settings for youth ages 12 to 18. In addition to education, discussion, and both individual and group counseling, the program offers training and consultation for faculty and parents.

Community Support Services: Psycho-education, school and community consultation, and interventions in the home, school, and other environmental settings to improve parenting, academic, daily living, and behavioral management skills.

Depression Intervention Program: Offering treatments unique to individuals to improve the quality of life of individuals and families struggling with mood disorders.

Crisis Intervention and Emergency Services: Crisis stabilization, conflict resolution, and telephone response 24 hours a day/7 days a week.
