 Description: The Psychiatric Institute of Washington (PIW) provides comprehensive behavioral healthcare for children, adolescents, adults and senior adults suffering from mental health and addictive illnesses. PIW is a short-term, acute care hospital offering inpatient, partial and intensive outpatient hospitalization, as well as specialized treatment programs for chemical dependency. PIW also runs several programs that aid those in need of services for mental health or addictive illnesses and those program services include the following: Adolescent services, Child services, Outpatient services (Day Center and Urgent Care Clinic), Psychiatric services, and the School.

The Adult Psychiatric Services Substance Abuse Unit is a 26 bed, short-term stabilization program for adults, ages 18-65+, with a primary diagnosis of chemical dependency and dual diagnosis/co-occurring disorder. Patients are assigned to one of our full-time psychiatrists and psychiatric social workers. All patients are expected to fully engage in the program schedule, which includes psycho-education, therapy groups, community meetings and goals groups. The average length of stay is 5 to 14 days, depending on the patient's individual needs and treatment plan. All patients are discharged with a plan for the next level of care, including aftercare appointments and housing referral, if needed.

PIW also accepts an Addiction Prevention and Recovery Administration (APRA) voucher program for detox, Medicare, D.C. Medicaid (under 18 or over 65), and private insurance.
