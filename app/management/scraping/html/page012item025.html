 Description: Miriam’s Kitchen is committed to ending chronic homelessness in DC by creating meaningful connections with chronically homeless individuals, placing them in permanent supportive housing, and ensuring they have the necessary support to remain in housing. Miriam’s Kitchen advocates for permanent supportive housing as a long-term solution, while meeting short-term needs by providing healthy meals and high-quality social services to more than 3,500 chronically homeless individuals each year.

EMERGENCY FOOD
Meals are served at Miriam's Kitchen every weekday. Breakfast is served between 6:30-8:00 am and dinner is served between 4:45-5:45 pm. Additionally, a light lunch is served during Miriam's Cafe every Wednesday between 12:00-2:00 pm. All of Miriam’s Kitchen’s food is made with fresh ingredients that include whole grains, fresh meats, and local and organic fruit and vegetables.

SOCIAL SERVICES
Miriam's Kitchen offers case management services, provides referrals to addiction and mental health services, information on employment and housing programs, free mailing address & voice-mail system. They also offer a weekly legal clinic (Wednesdays from 7:30-8:30 am), psychiatric services on Thursdays from 6:30-9:30 am, health care services on Fridays from 7:45-9:15 am, and blood pressure checks. Nearest Metro Station: Foggy Bottom Metro Station.

The legal clinic is entitled, "The Washington Legal Clinic for the Homeless." It provides representation in all civil legal matters (i.e. public benefits, housing, employment, immigration, impact litigation, legislative and policy-related issues).

VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITIES
Volunteers help serve breakfast, lunch, and dinner.
