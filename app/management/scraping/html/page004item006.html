 Description: The Edward C. Mazique Parent Child Center (ECMPCC) is a non-profit community based organization that provides home based services to pregnant women and their children from birth to age three. ECMPCC offers an Early Head Start program, designed for families with children under 3 and families have an option for service delivery either in their homes or in one of the ECMPCC centers. The ECMPCC also offers a Head Start program for children between the ages of 3-5 years. Children are provided with on-site mental health counseling services; there are also on-site speech, language, physical and occupational therapies, which are provided by private vendors; an on-site health suite with a Nutritionist/Dietician and with a pediatrician that visits once a week.

The home-based program has a prenatal focus which provides a specialized curriculum to address pregnancy and related issues; and the Men of Mazique/Men on a Mission group is a support for males and encourage male involvement and fatherhood development program.

Services eligibility is for low income families; Pregnant teens and women; Families with children from infancy to five years old; and the targeted communities are: Wards 1, 2, 4, and 5 (Early Head Start); Citywide (Head Start); and Children with special needs.
