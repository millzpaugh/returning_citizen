 Description: Father McKenna Center Inc. was established in 1983 to carry on the legacy of Father Horace McKenna, a Jesuit priest who devoted his life to the service of the poor and homeless. The Father McKenna Center can either provide or efficiently refer guests to any service a homeless man needs. The Father McKenna Center is the last day-time drop-in center for homeless men in the downtown Washington, DC area. The Father McKenna Center also serves men, women and families through its programs that include:

The Men's day-time drop-in center; Food pantry serving families and senior citizens; Rent and utility assistance to prevent homelessness; Cold weather hypothermia shelter to prevent homeless men from freezing to death on our streets; and A small transitional living program.

HOUSING Father McKenna Center provides overnight shelter for homeless men during hypothermia season (November through April). Men contribute to the upkeep of the facility.

SOCIAL SERVICES
The shelter offers case management and counseling. Men attend a day program daily (NA/AA meetings).Lunch is provided for those attending.

Clothing distribution occurs on Tuesdays from 8:00 am - 10:00 am. Showers are offered 8:30 am - 10:45 am. Laundry services are offered Monday - Friday. Rent and utility services are offered for Ward 6 residents only.

EMERGENCY FOOD
Emergency food is offered for Ward 6 residents only.

Nearest Metro station: Union Station and nearest Metro bus lines: 80, 96 & X2.
